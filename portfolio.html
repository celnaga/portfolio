01 — Retail Pricing
Project 01

Axiom.

"Stop subsidizing your loyal customers."

A prescriptive pricing intelligence engine for Chinese social commerce platforms (Xiaohongshu, Douyin) that moves beyond binary discount decisions. K-Means clustering segments customers by behavioral attributes; logistic regression estimates individual price sensitivity; a Gurobi-powered optimization model determines the profit-maximising discount for each segment — balancing conversion uplift against margin erosion at scale.

Python K-Means Clustering Logistic Regression Gurobi Optimization
Stage 01 — Behavioural Clustering

Who are your customers?

Unsupervised segmentation of 100,000 users into 4 archetypes using age, purchase frequency, total spend, social following, and platform tenure. K=4 chosen via the elbow method.

Stage 02 — Intent Forecasting

Who will actually buy?

Predicts purchase probability per segment at each discount tier. Key finding: is_follow_author (coef 0.83) dominates. Creator trust beats price sensitivity in social commerce.

Stage 03 — Value Optimisation

What's the best strategy?

Binary integer programme finds the optimal discount allocation across all segment × category pairs, subject to a ¥1,000,000 budget cap and stock constraints.

02 — A/B Testing
Project 02

Spotify
Friend Badge

"Does social proof drive listening — and ultimately, conversion?"

Designed and analysed a randomised controlled experiment testing whether a "Your friends are listening to this" badge on Spotify's Home feed drives higher engagement and Premium conversion among free-tier UK users. Synthetic dataset of 117,950 users, 50/50 user-level RCT, two-sample t-tests, OLS with HC3 robust standard errors, and subgroup heterogeneity analysis.

Python Statistical Testing OLS Regression Causal Inference
Minutes Streamed Lift +50.3% p < 0.001 · 95% CI: +5.07 to +5.56 min
Premium Conversion Lift +0.96pp 3.04% → 4.01% · p < 0.001
Effect Persistence 4 wks ~47–50% above control through day 28
App Uninstall Guardrail −0.19pp No user experience degradation

Why the effect is causal

Randomisation check confirmed balance across age (p=0.748) and mutual friends (p=0.55). The treatment coefficient of 5.31 min holds with and without controls — the badge is the driver, not confounding covariates.

74.5% of the lift is generated directly from on-card listening, with +1.35 min spillover to the broader app — suggesting a genuine expansion of total engagement, not just redistribution.

Targeting insight: stronger graphs respond more

Relative lift by friend bucket — users with 51–200 mutual friends show a statistically significant additional response above baseline (p=0.012). This points to a clear roll-out strategy: start with high-connectivity users.

3–10 friends +48.3%
11–50 friends +53.4%
51–200 friends +73.2%
Recommendation

Scale progressively to the full UK free-tier base, starting with users with 51+ mutual friends. Keep minutes streamed as the lead metric for follow-up tests. Use dismissal rate and uninstall rate as rollout guardrails.

Project 03

The Postcode
Premium.

London's £630M Property Divide — location specificity over borough names.

Challenged the conventional assumption that London's property price variation is primarily a postcode-level phenomenon. Through granular spatial visualisations built in Tableau, the analysis reveals that price dispersion within individual postcodes can rival — and in some cases exceed — variation across postcode boundaries.
